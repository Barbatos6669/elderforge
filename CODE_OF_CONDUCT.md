# Code of Conduct

Elderforge should be a serious, welcoming place to build. Contributors are expected to treat each other with patience, honesty, and respect.

## Expected Behavior

- Be kind and direct.
- Critique ideas and code, not people.
- Assume good intent, but accept responsibility when something lands poorly.
- Make room for new contributors to learn.
- Keep discussions focused on the project.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, or personal attacks.
- Racist, sexist, homophobic, transphobic, ableist, or otherwise demeaning language.
- Sharing private information without permission.
- Repeated disruption after maintainers ask for a change in behavior.
- Submitting copied or infringing material.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove comments, close issues, reject contributions, or restrict participation when behavior harms the project or its contributors. Serious concerns should be reported privately to the maintainers once a contact address is published.
