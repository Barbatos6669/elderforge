# Hearthvale Starter Creatures

These are early creature and animal concepts for the Hearthvale playtest area.
They are lore-first, but each entry includes gameplay hooks so art, combat,
gathering, drops, quests, and the glossary can grow from the same source.

## Design Goals

- Make Hearthvale feel alive before the player meets major monsters.
- Give hunting, skinning, alchemy, and combat simple early targets.
- Keep normal animals, corrupted variants, and magical creatures distinct.
- Support the monster-knowledge loop from the lore bible: observe, fight,
  gather, learn weaknesses, and unlock better preparation.

## Hollowfield Rat

Small rats common around abandoned farms, granaries, broken mills, and old
cellars. They are not monsters, but they thrive where people stop cleaning and
start whispering about curses.

### In-Game Description

**Hollowfield Rat**

A lean field rat with pale whiskers and sharp black eyes. Hollowfield rats are
ordinary animals, but their nests often mark forgotten grain stores, rotten
floorboards, or places where people left in a hurry.

### Gameplay Hooks

- Role: T1 skinnable creature and early combat target.
- Behavior: Wanders, flees if possible, bites if cornered.
- Drops: Rat Hide, small meat, cracked tooth, spoiled grain.
- Weaknesses: Steel, traps, fire, loud noise.
- Knowledge unlocks:
  - 10: Rats often reveal hidden cellars or food stores.
  - 25: Rat nests can point toward ruin loot.
  - 50: Diseased rats should be burned, not butchered.

## Hearthvale Hare

Hares live in the wheat edges, low hedgerows, and grass around Hearthbridge.
Children are told not to chase the white-eared ones, because those are said to
belong to the Old Groves.

### In-Game Description

**Hearthvale Hare**

A quick brown hare with bright eyes and long ears. It is common enough to hunt,
but hard enough to catch that new hunters learn patience before they learn
pride.

### Gameplay Hooks

- Role: Passive small-game animal.
- Behavior: Flees from players, pauses near brush, uses short burst movement.
- Drops: Small Hide, hare meat, lucky foot, soft fur.
- Weaknesses: Traps, bows, stealth approach.
- Knowledge unlocks:
  - 10: Hares flee toward nearby brush.
  - 25: Quiet approach increases hunting success.
  - 50: White-eared hares may mark Groveborn shrine paths.

## Vale Deer

Vale Deer are common in Hearthvale's woods and meadow lines. The Verdant Circle
teaches that taking one deer is hunting, but scattering the herd is arrogance.

### In-Game Description

**Vale Deer**

A lean forest deer with warm brown fur and pale throat markings. It listens more
than it looks, and by the time most hunters see it clearly, it has already
decided where to run.

### Gameplay Hooks

- Role: T2 hide source and tracking tutorial animal.
- Behavior: Passive, alert, herd-aware, flees when approached.
- Drops: Deer Hide, venison, antler, sinew.
- Weaknesses: Bows, traps, terrain funneling.
- Knowledge unlocks:
  - 10: Deer tracks show recent movement direction.
  - 25: Wind direction matters while stalking deer.
  - 50: Antlers can be used in basic charm and bow recipes.

## Ashback Boar

Ashback Boars are Hearthvale boars with dark bristles along the spine. Old
farmers say the stripe came from the ashfall after the Sundering. Whether true
or not, the animals are stubborn enough to make a priest swear.

### In-Game Description

**Ashback Boar**

A heavy boar with a dark ash-colored stripe down its back and small tusks
polished by rooting through clay and stone. It is not evil, but it is very easy
to insult.

### Gameplay Hooks

- Role: Early hostile animal and hunting danger.
- Behavior: Warns first, charges if approached, retreats when badly wounded.
- Drops: Boar Hide, tusk, tough meat, bristle bundle.
- Weaknesses: Spears, sidesteps, traps, attacking after a missed charge.
- Knowledge unlocks:
  - 10: Boars telegraph charges with hoof scraping.
  - 25: Side movement helps avoid the first hit.
  - 50: Tusks can reinforce simple tool handles.

## Lantern Moth

Lantern Moths gather around old lamps, moonleaf patches, shrine stones, and
lantern oil spills. Lantern Wardens say their wings hold the memory of safe
roads, which is why the moths drift toward paths where people used to travel.

### In-Game Description

**Lantern Moth**

A soft-winged moth with a faint gold glow under its wings. It is harmless, but
its dust is useful in weak light potions, trail markers, and beginner warding
mixtures.

### Gameplay Hooks

- Role: Night ambience, alchemy gatherable, and tutorial for noncombat harvests.
- Behavior: Floats near light sources, scatters when players sprint nearby.
- Drops: Lantern dust, delicate wing, tiny glow sac.
- Weaknesses: Smoke, cold, covered lanterns.
- Knowledge unlocks:
  - 10: Lantern Moths gather near moonleaf and old paths.
  - 25: Their dust can mark trails for a short time.
  - 50: Unusual swarms may reveal a hidden shrine or fresh spirit trace.

## Claybank Toad

Claybank Toads live in Hearthvale ditches, river bends, and muddy clay pits.
They are ugly in the trustworthy way of old pond creatures. Apothecaries like
them more than farmers do.

### In-Game Description

**Claybank Toad**

A squat brown toad with clay-colored skin and a pale throat pouch. It croaks
before rain and carries a mild toxin useful for early antidote practice.

### Gameplay Hooks

- Role: Starter poison, antidote, and wetlands tutorial creature.
- Behavior: Hops away slowly, releases weak toxin if grabbed or struck.
- Drops: Toad gland, muddy skin, bitter tongue, pond slime.
- Weaknesses: Nets, careful handling, clean water.
- Knowledge unlocks:
  - 10: Toads gather before rain.
  - 25: Their glands can be used in weak poison or weak antidote recipes.
  - 50: Toad clusters may indicate hidden clay or herb nodes nearby.

## Mineweb Spider

Mineweb Spiders are common in shallow mines and abandoned cellars near
Hearthvale. Most are natural cave hunters, but nests near Rift-touched stone can
turn aggressive and clever.

### In-Game Description

**Mineweb Spider**

A low cave spider with gray legs, pale eyes, and sticky webbing stretched
between cracked stones. It waits where careless miners put their hands.

### Gameplay Hooks

- Role: First cave ambusher and slow-effect tutorial enemy.
- Behavior: Hides near corners, spits web to slow, retreats toward nests.
- Drops: Spider silk, venom sac, chitin shard.
- Weaknesses: Fire, boots, ranged attacks, breaking web anchors.
- Knowledge unlocks:
  - 10: Webbed corners may hide spider nests.
  - 25: Fire clears weak webs quickly.
  - 50: Venom sacs can make early poison coatings.
  - 75: Rift-touched nests produce more aggressive spiders.

## Duskfeather Crow

Duskfeather Crows follow plows, battlefields, gallows, and abandoned roads.
Hearthbridge children are told that if a crow watches you too long, you should
check whether you are standing on someone else's secret.

### In-Game Description

**Duskfeather Crow**

A dark crow with blue-black feathers and a sharp voice. It is no monster, but
it has an uncomfortable habit of finding trouble before people do.

### Gameplay Hooks

- Role: Ambient scavenger, tracking clue, and ruin hint.
- Behavior: Perches above bodies, camps, and hidden caches; flees when approached.
- Drops: Dusk feather, small bone, stolen trinket from rare nests.
- Weaknesses: Bows, thrown stones, bait.
- Knowledge unlocks:
  - 10: Crows often gather near recent kills or abandoned camps.
  - 25: Following a startled crow can reveal a nearby corpse or cache.
  - 50: Blue-black feathers can be used in simple omen charms.

## Corrupted Wolf

Normal wolves belong in Hearthvale's food chain. Corrupted Wolves do not. Their
fur knots around black root splinters, their eyes glow ember-yellow, and they
keep hunting after hunger should have ended.

### In-Game Description

**Corrupted Wolf**

A Hearthvale wolf warped by Rift-scarred root corruption. It still hunts like a
wolf, but the pack no longer kills only to eat.

### Gameplay Hooks

- Role: First serious hostile beast.
- Behavior: Pack hunter, circles weak targets, howls to pull nearby wolves.
- Drops: Wolf Hide, corrupted fang, blackroot splinter, tainted meat.
- Weaknesses: Fire, steel, traps, separating the pack.
- Knowledge unlocks:
  - 10: Corrupted wolves rarely travel alone.
  - 25: Fire interrupts pack pressure.
  - 50: Blackroot splinters can reveal the nearby corruption source.
  - 75: Killing the pack leader weakens the rest.

## Blackroot Stag

A Blackroot Stag begins as a normal Vale Deer that drinks from a corrupted
stream or grazes too near a Rift-scarred root. Its antlers darken, its breath
steams even in summer, and the herd will not follow it.

### In-Game Description

**Blackroot Stag**

A corrupted stag with black antlers and rootlike growths beneath its hide. It
moves like a wounded forest trying to gore the thing that hurt it.

### Gameplay Hooks

- Role: Rare corrupted field threat and early mini-boss candidate.
- Behavior: Charges in straight lines, scrapes the ground, calls nearby wolves.
- Drops: Deer Hide, blackroot antler, corrupted sinew, tainted venison.
- Weaknesses: Fire, spears, terrain, cleansing oils.
- Knowledge unlocks:
  - 10: Blackroot Stags usually mark a nearby corrupted water source.
  - 25: Their charge is dangerous but predictable.
  - 50: Blackroot antlers can strengthen anti-corruption charms.
  - 75: Cleansing the water source prevents future stag corruption.

## Silverneedle Needlekin

Needlekin are tiny forest spirits made of pine needles, pale bark, moonlit sap,
and old promises. They are not evil. They attack gatherers who cut too much,
cut corrupted trees carelessly, or forget the old rule: take the trunk, leave
the cone.

### In-Game Description

**Silverneedle Needlekin**

A small grove spirit with a bark mask, needle-cloak, and eyes like moonlit sap.
It remembers oaths spoken beneath Silverneedle branches and does not forgive
wasteful cutting quickly.

### Gameplay Hooks

- Role: Magical forest spirit and gathering consequence.
- Behavior: Defensive, appears near abused groves, throws needle bursts.
- Drops: Silverneedle Cones, Silverpine Resin, Moonlit Resin from rare variants.
- Weaknesses: Fire, steel, apology/offering rituals, cutting corruption roots.
- Knowledge unlocks:
  - 10: Needlekin attack wasteful gatherers first.
  - 25: Pinecone offerings can calm some Needlekin.
  - 50: Moonlit Needlekin may guard rare resin.
  - 75: Corrupted Needlekin can be purified instead of killed.

## Quest Hooks

### First Hunt

A Hearthbridge cook needs meat and hides for the town. The player tracks
Hearthvale Hare, learns to approach quietly, then returns with enough small
game to feed the workers.

### The Boar in the Clay

An Ashback Boar has been rooting through clay pits and breaking marker stakes.
The player must drive it off or kill it, teaching charge timing and animal
aggression.

### Lights in the Ditch

Lantern Moths are gathering around a muddy drainage ditch outside Hearthbridge.
The player collects moth dust, finds Claybank Toads, and learns the first weak
poison and antidote recipes.

### Webs Below the Mill

The cellar beneath an abandoned mill is full of Mineweb Spider nests. The
player burns weak webs, fights spiders, and gathers silk for early crafting.

### Teeth in the Wheat

Corrupted Wolves have started hunting near the fields. The player investigates
tracks, defeats the pack, and finds blackroot splinters pointing toward a
corrupted grove.

### The Stag That Would Not Run

Hunters report a Vale Deer that does not flee. The player tracks the animal,
discovers a Blackroot Stag, and follows its trail back to corrupted water.

### Take the Trunk, Leave the Cone

After too much careless logging, Needlekin appear near a Silverneedle grove.
The player can fight them, leave a pinecone offering, or find and cut the
corrupted root heart stirring them up.

## Naming Notes

- `hide_t1` currently maps to **Wolf Hide I** in the resource alignment doc, but
  the rat playtest can keep yielding the same gameplay id until dedicated rat
  or small-hide resources are added.
- `hide_t2` is reserved for **Deer Hide II**.
- Needlekin are tied to Silverneedle Pine, which is a Frostspine magical wood in
  the current alignment, not the Hearthvale T1 oak starter tree.
